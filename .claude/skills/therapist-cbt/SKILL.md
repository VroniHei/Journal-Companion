# CBT — Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

**Most AI-ready therapeutic modality.** Highly structured, clear protocols, evidence-based.

---

## Core Concept

```
Thoughts → Feelings → Behaviors

Distorted thoughts cause emotional distress and unhelpful behaviors.
Identify the distortion → Challenge it → Replace it → Feel better → Act better.
```

---

## Session Structure

Every CBT session follows this flow:

```
1. CHECK-IN
   "How are you feeling today? Rate your mood 1-10."
   "What's been going on since we last talked?"

2. HOMEWORK REVIEW (returning users)
   "Last time we worked on [X]. How did that go?"
   "Did you try the thought record exercise?"

3. AGENDA SETTING
   "What feels most important to focus on today?"

4. INTERVENTION (pick one based on what comes up)
   → Cognitive Restructuring (most common)
   → Behavioral Activation
   → Thought Record
   → Exposure Planning

5. SUMMARY
   "Here's what we covered today..."

6. HOMEWORK
   "For this week, try..."

7. FEEDBACK
   "How was this session for you?"
```

## The 12 Cognitive Distortions

When a user describes a negative thought, classify which distortion(s) are present:

| # | Distortion | Pattern | Example |
|---|-----------|---------|---------|
| 1 | All-or-nothing thinking | Black/white, no middle ground | "If I'm not perfect, I'm a total failure" |
| 2 | Overgeneralization | One event = always/never | "I failed once, I'll always fail" |
| 3 | Mental filter | Dwell on one negative, ignore positives | "My boss praised 5 things but criticized 1, so the day was terrible" |
| 4 | Disqualifying the positive | Discount good things | "They only said that to be nice" |
| 5 | Jumping to conclusions | Assume negative outcome without evidence | "They didn't text back, they must hate me" |
| 6 | Mind reading | Assume you know what others think | "Everyone at the party thought I was awkward" |
| 7 | Fortune telling | Predict the worst | "The interview will go terribly" |
| 8 | Magnification/Minimization | Blow up negatives, shrink positives | "My mistake was catastrophic, but my success was luck" |
| 9 | Emotional reasoning | Feelings = facts | "I feel stupid, therefore I am stupid" |
| 10 | Should statements | Rigid rules about how things must be | "I should always be productive" |
| 11 | Labeling | Apply global labels to self/others | "I'm a loser" instead of "I made a mistake" |
| 12 | Personalization | Blame yourself for things outside your control | "The team project failed because of me" |

## Cognitive Restructuring (Core Technique)

### The Process:

```
1. IDENTIFY the thought
   "What thought went through your mind when you felt that way?"
   "What's the story you're telling yourself about this?"

2. IDENTIFY the distortion
   "That sounds like [distortion name]. Does that resonate?"

3. EXAMINE the evidence
   "What evidence supports this thought?"
   "What evidence goes against it?"
   "If your best friend told you they were thinking this, what would you say?"

4. REFRAME
   "Can we find a more balanced way to look at this?"
   "Not necessarily positive — just more accurate."

5. TEST (homework)
   "How could we test whether this thought is true?"
```

### Socratic Questions (Use These):

- "What's the evidence for and against this thought?"
- "Is there another way to look at this situation?"
- "What would you tell a friend who had this thought?"
- "What's the worst that could happen? How likely is it really?"
- "Are you confusing a thought with a fact?"
- "Will this matter in 5 years?"
- "What would [someone you respect] say about this?"

## Thought Record Exercise

Guide users through a written thought record:

```
SITUATION: What happened?
  → "I was presenting and stumbled over my words."

EMOTION: What did you feel? (1-10 intensity)
  → "Embarrassed (8/10), Anxious (7/10)"

AUTOMATIC THOUGHT: What went through your mind?
  → "Everyone thinks I'm incompetent."

EVIDENCE FOR: What supports this thought?
  → "I did stumble. Some people looked uncomfortable."

EVIDENCE AGAINST: What goes against this?
  → "My manager said it was a good presentation. 
     I've done many successful presentations before. 
     Most people probably didn't even notice."

BALANCED THOUGHT: A more realistic perspective
  → "I stumbled once in an otherwise solid presentation. 
     One mistake doesn't define my competence."

OUTCOME: How do you feel now?
  → "Embarrassed (3/10), more accepting"
```

## Behavioral Activation

For users stuck in avoidance/depression cycles:

```
1. ACTIVITY MONITORING
   "For the next few days, just notice what you do each hour 
    and rate your mood (1-10) during each activity."

2. IDENTIFY PATTERNS
   "Which activities make you feel better? Which make you feel worse?"

3. VALUES-BASED ACTIVITY SELECTION
   "What matters to you? What did you used to enjoy?"

4. GRADED TASK ASSIGNMENT
   Start small. "Can you take a 5-minute walk today?"
   Not: "Go run a marathon."

5. SCHEDULE
   "Let's put one pleasant activity in your calendar this week."
```

## Response Style for CBT

- **Guide, don't lecture** — ask questions rather than explaining
- **Name the distortion gently** — "It sounds like that might be fortune telling" not "That's fortune telling, you're wrong"
- **Validate first** — "That thought feels really real when you're in it"
- **Be concrete** — use their specific examples, not abstract concepts
- **Homework is collaborative** — "Would you be willing to try..." not "Your homework is..."

---

## What CBT Is NOT Good For

- Active crisis → safety protocol first
- Deep trauma processing → recommend EMDR/IFS/somatic therapist
- Personality disorders → DBT more appropriate
- Philosophical/existential concerns → ACT may fit better
- People who resist structured approaches → more exploratory modalities
