# DBT — Dialectical Behavior Therapy Skills

**Best for:** Emotional overwhelm, crisis survival, interpersonal difficulties, difficulty regulating intense emotions.

**Note:** Full DBT requires a therapist + skills group + phone coaching. This skill provides the SKILLS COMPONENT only — not full DBT treatment.

---

## Core Philosophy

- Balance acceptance AND change (the dialectic)
- Some people have intense emotional reactions — that's biological, not weakness
- Skills can be learned to manage those reactions
- Validate the emotion, then work on changing the response

---

## Four Skill Modules

### 1. Mindfulness (Foundation)

**"What" Skills:**
- **Observe** — Notice without reacting. "What am I feeling right now?"
- **Describe** — Put words to it. "I notice tension in my chest and a thought that says 'I can't do this'"
- **Participate** — Fully engage in the present moment

**"How" Skills:**
- **Non-judgmentally** — Drop "should" and "shouldn't." Feelings aren't good or bad, they just are
- **One-mindfully** — One thing at a time. Not "eat lunch AND scroll AND worry"
- **Effectively** — Do what works, not what's "right"

**Quick Exercise: 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding**
```
"Name 5 things you can see right now."
"4 things you can touch."
"3 things you can hear."
"2 things you can smell."
"1 thing you can taste."
```

### 2. Distress Tolerance (Crisis Survival)

**When to use:** Emotional crisis. You can't fix the situation right now. You need to SURVIVE without making it worse.

#### STOP Skill
```
S — Stop. Don't react.
T — Take a step back. Physically if possible.
O — Observe. What's happening? What am I feeling?
P — Proceed mindfully. What's the wise thing to do?
```

#### TIPP Skill (Body-Based Crisis Intervention)
```
T — Temperature: Splash cold water on face, hold ice cubes, cold shower
     (Activates dive reflex, slows heart rate)

I — Intense exercise: 20 jumping jacks, run stairs, push-ups
     (Burns off stress hormones)

P — Paced breathing: In 4 counts, hold 4, out 8
     (Activates parasympathetic nervous system)

P — Paired muscle relaxation: Tense each muscle group 5 sec, release
     (Progressive relaxation)
```

#### ACCEPTS (Distraction)
```
A — Activities: Do something absorbing
C — Contributing: Help someone else
C — Comparisons: Compare to worse times you've survived
E — Emotions: Generate opposite emotion (comedy, music)
P — Pushing away: Temporarily shelve the problem
T — Thoughts: Count, puzzles, mental games
S — Sensations: Ice, hot tea, strong mint, loud music
```

#### Self-Soothe with 5 Senses
```
Vision: Look at nature, art, photos
Hearing: Music, nature sounds, ASMR
Smell: Candles, essential oils, cooking
Taste: Comfort food (mindfully), tea, strong flavors
Touch: Soft blanket, warm bath, pet an animal
```

### 3. Emotion Regulation

#### ABC PLEASE (Reducing Vulnerability)
```
A — Accumulate positives: Do pleasant things daily
B — Build mastery: Do one thing that builds competence
C — Cope ahead: Rehearse handling difficult situations

PL — Treat Physical illness
E — Eat balanced meals
A — Avoid mood-altering substances
S — Sleep hygiene
E — Exercise
```

#### Opposite Action
When the emotion doesn't fit the facts OR acting on it isn't effective:

| Emotion | Urge | Opposite Action |
|---------|------|-----------------|
| Fear/Anxiety | Avoid | Approach, face it |
| Sadness | Withdraw | Get active, engage |
| Anger | Attack | Be gentle, avoid |
| Shame (unjustified) | Hide | Share, be visible |
| Guilt (unjustified) | Confess/apologize | Don't apologize, let go |

#### Check the Facts
```
"What emotion am I feeling?"
"What event triggered it?"
"What am I thinking/interpreting about the event?"
"Does my emotion FIT THE FACTS? (not my interpretation)"
"Is acting on this emotion EFFECTIVE?"
```

### 4. Interpersonal Effectiveness

#### DEAR MAN (Asking for What You Need)
```
D — Describe the situation (just facts)
E — Express how you feel ("I feel...")
A — Assert what you want (be specific)
R — Reinforce ("This would help because...")

M — Mindful (stay on topic, don't get derailed)
A — Appear confident (even if you're not)
N — Negotiate (be willing to give to get)
```

#### GIVE (Maintaining Relationships)
```
G — Gentle (no attacks, threats, or judgments)
I — Interested (listen, be curious)
V — Validate ("I can see why you'd feel that way")
E — Easy manner (lighten up, use humor)
```

#### FAST (Maintaining Self-Respect)
```
F — Fair (to yourself AND the other person)
A — No Apologies (for existing, having an opinion, asking)
S — Stick to values (don't sell out to be liked)
T — Truthful (don't exaggerate, make excuses, or minimize)
```

## Session Flow for DBT Skills

```
1. CHECK-IN: Current emotional state, any crises this week
2. DIARY CARD REVIEW: What skills did they use? What worked?
3. SKILL TEACHING: Introduce/practice one skill
4. PRACTICE: Role-play or real-world application
5. HOMEWORK: Practice the skill this week, track on diary card
```

---

## When DBT Fits

- Intense emotions that feel out of control
- Self-harm urges or history
- Relationship chaos
- Impulsive behaviors
- Difficulty tolerating distress
- "I feel everything SO intensely"

## When DBT Doesn't Fit

- Client needs depth/exploration → psychodynamic, IFS
- Mild stress → CBT or SFBT is sufficient
- Client intellectualizes → needs somatic approach
- Active substance dependence → needs specialized treatment first

---

## Response Style for DBT

- **Validate first, always** — "That makes sense given what you've been through"
- **Normalize intensity** — "Some people feel things more deeply, that's not wrong"
- **Practical and concrete** — give specific skills, not abstract concepts
- **Non-judgmental** — about urges, behaviors, emotions
- **Dialectical** — "Two things can be true. You can be struggling AND capable."
