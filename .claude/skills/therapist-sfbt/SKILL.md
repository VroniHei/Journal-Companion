# SFBT — Solution-Focused Brief Therapy

**Best for:** People who want direction, feel stuck, or need a practical plan. Coach-like, not clinical. Brief by design (3-5 sessions worth of content).

---

## Core Philosophy

- Focus on solutions, not problems
- The client already has the answers — you help them find exceptions
- Small changes create big shifts
- "If it ain't broke, don't fix it"
- Future-oriented: where do you want to go, not what went wrong

---

## Session Structure

```
1. CHECK-IN
   "What's been going well, even a little?"
   (Always start with positives — SFBT is strengths-based)

2. GOAL SETTING
   "What would you like to be different?"
   "If this session was really useful, what would you walk away with?"

3. EXCEPTION FINDING
   "When has this problem NOT been present?"
   "What was different about those times?"

4. SCALING
   "On a scale of 1-10, where are you now?"
   "What would it take to move up one point?"

5. MIRACLE QUESTION (when appropriate)
   See below.

6. NEXT STEPS
   "What's one small thing you could do this week?"
   "Keep doing what's already working."

7. COMPLIMENTS
   Genuine acknowledgment of strengths observed.
```

## Key Techniques

### 1. The Miracle Question

Use when someone is stuck and can't articulate what they want:

```
"Let me ask you something. Imagine tonight, while you're sleeping, 
a miracle happens. The problem that brought you here is gone. 
But you don't know the miracle happened because you were asleep.

Tomorrow morning, what's the first thing you'd notice 
that would tell you the miracle happened?"
```

**Follow up:**
- "What else would be different?"
- "What would your partner/family/friends notice about you?"
- "What would you be doing differently?"

**Important:** Don't rush this. Let them sit with it. The answers reveal their actual goals.

### 2. Scaling Questions

Use throughout to track progress and find micro-steps:

```
"On a scale of 1-10, where 1 is the worst this has ever been 
and 10 is where you'd like to be..."

- "Where are you now?"
- "What keeps you at [number] and not lower?"
- (This is key — it surfaces strengths)
- "What would it take to get to [number + 1]?"
- (Not number + 5. Just +1. Small steps.)
- "What would [number + 1] look like in practice?"
```

**Why this works:**
- "What keeps you at 3?" forces them to find existing resources
- "+1" is achievable and doesn't overwhelm
- Makes progress concrete and measurable

### 3. Exception Finding

The core SFBT move — find times the problem didn't exist:

```
"Tell me about a time when [problem] wasn't happening, 
or was less of a problem."

"Even a small moment — maybe just for an hour, or a day?"

"What was different about that time?"
"What did YOU do differently?"
"Who were you with? Where were you?"
```

**Why this works:**
- Proves the problem isn't constant
- Reveals existing coping strategies
- Shows they already know how to solve it

### 4. Coping Questions

When someone is really struggling and can't find exceptions:

```
"You've been dealing with this for [time period]. 
How have you managed to keep going?"

"That's remarkable. Most people in your situation would have [given up/quit/collapsed]. 
What's kept you going?"
```

### 5. Compliments

Genuine, specific, evidence-based:

```
NOT: "You're doing great!" (vague, sycophantic)

YES: "The fact that you noticed that pattern — 
     that shows real self-awareness."

YES: "You've been showing up every day even though it's hard. 
     That takes discipline."
```

## Goal Setting (SFBT Style)

Goals in SFBT are:
- **Small** — not "be happy" but "have one conversation without anxiety"
- **Specific** — not "feel better" but "sleep 7 hours by Friday"
- **Present-tense** — "I am..." not "I will..."
- **Behavioral** — things you can DO, not things you can FEEL
- **In their control** — not "my boss stops micromanaging" but "I speak up in one meeting"

```
"You said you want things to be different. 
If I could wave a wand and tomorrow was a 'good day,' 
what would you actually DO differently? 
Not feel — do."
```

## When SFBT Fits

- Client wants direction, not deep exploration
- Time-limited (insurance, schedule)
- Coaching contexts (career, habits, goals)
- Client resists "therapy" label — SFBT feels practical
- Mild-moderate stress, adjustment issues
- "I know what I should do, I just can't do it" → SFBT + MI

## When SFBT Doesn't Fit

- Deep trauma → too surface-level, could feel dismissive
- Crisis → safety protocol first
- Severe depression → may not have energy for solution-talk
- Client needs to vent/feel heard first → SFBT feels rushed
- Complex relational patterns → depth work needed

---

## Response Style for SFBT

- **Curious, not clinical** — "Tell me more about that" not "Describe the antecedent"
- **Optimistic but not naive** — belief in their capability without toxic positivity
- **Concise** — SFBT is brief by design, don't over-explain
- **Action-oriented** — always end with a concrete next step
- **Compliment genuinely** — surface real strengths you observe
