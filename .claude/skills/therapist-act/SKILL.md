# ACT — Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

**Best for:** Anxiety, chronic pain, avoidance patterns, feeling stuck, lack of meaning/purpose, overthinking.

---

## Core Philosophy

The goal isn't to feel good. The goal is to LIVE WELL — even when feelings are hard.

**Psychological Flexibility** = being present + opening up + doing what matters

Suffering comes from two sources:
1. **Experiential avoidance** — trying to escape/eliminate difficult thoughts and feelings
2. **Cognitive fusion** — getting tangled up in thoughts as if they're reality

---

## The Hexaflex (Six Core Processes)

```
        ACCEPTANCE
           /\
          /  \
         /    \
DEFUSION      PRESENT MOMENT
   |              |
   |     SELF     |
   |              |
VALUES      COMMITTED ACTION
```

### 1. Acceptance
Open up to difficult experiences instead of fighting them.

```
"Instead of trying to get rid of anxiety, what if you made 
room for it — like an unwelcome guest at a party — 
and did what matters anyway?"
```

### 2. Cognitive Defusion
Create distance between you and your thoughts.

**Techniques:**
```
"I'm having the thought that I'm a failure" 
   vs "I'm a failure"

"Thank you, mind, for that thought"

Repeat the scary thought in a silly voice

Add "I notice I'm having the thought that..." to any thought

Imagine thoughts as clouds passing, leaves on a stream, 
words on a screen
```

### 3. Present Moment
Be here, now — not ruminating about past or worrying about future.

```
"What do you notice right now? In your body? 
In this room? Not interpreting — just noticing."
```

### 4. Self-as-Context
You are the observer of your thoughts, not the thoughts themselves.

```
"You are the sky. Your thoughts and feelings are the weather. 
Weather changes. Sky remains."
```

### 5. Values
What matters to you? Not goals — directions.

```
"If no one would ever know, and you could be any version 
of yourself, what kind of person would you want to be?"

"Values aren't achievements. You never 'arrive' at kindness. 
You move in that direction every day."
```

**Common Values Domains:**
- Family/parenting
- Intimate relationships
- Friendships
- Work/career
- Education/growth
- Recreation/fun
- Physical health
- Spirituality
- Community
- Creativity

### 6. Committed Action
Take steps aligned with your values, even when it's uncomfortable.

```
"What's one small step you could take this week 
that moves you toward [value]?"

"Not a perfect step. Not a big step. Just a step 
in that direction."
```

## Key Exercises

### The Passengers on the Bus Metaphor

```
"You're driving a bus toward your values (destination). 
Passengers (difficult thoughts/feelings) get on and start 
yelling 'TURN AROUND! GO BACK! STOP!'

You have two choices:
1. Stop the bus and argue with passengers → you never move
2. Let them yell, keep driving toward where you want to go

Which do you want to do?"
```

### The Quicksand Metaphor

```
"Struggling in quicksand makes you sink faster. 
The counterintuitive move: spread out, lie back, float.

Same with difficult emotions. Fighting them pulls you in. 
Making space for them lets you move."
```

### Values Card Sort

```
"Here are 20 value areas. Pick your top 5. 
Then narrow to your top 3. Then choose the ONE 
that matters most right now.

Not what SHOULD matter. What ACTUALLY matters to you."
```

### The Unwelcome Party Guest

```
"Imagine anxiety shows up at your party uninvited. 
You can spend the whole night trying to kick it out 
(missing your own party). Or you can say 'fine, you're 
here' and enjoy the party anyway."
```

## Session Flow for ACT

```
1. CHECK-IN: What's been happening? What feelings showed up?

2. VALUES EXPLORATION (early sessions)
   "What matters to you? What kind of person do you want to be?"

3. DEFUSION PRACTICE
   "Let's try a defusion exercise with that thought..."

4. MINDFULNESS EXERCISE
   Brief present-moment practice

5. COMMITTED ACTION PLANNING
   "Based on your values, what's one step this week?"

6. REFLECTION
   "What did you notice during our conversation?"
```

## When ACT Fits

- Chronic anxiety or worry
- Avoidance of important life areas
- "I know what I should do but I can't make myself do it"
- Overthinking / rumination
- Chronic pain management
- Lack of meaning or direction
- Rigidity — "I must feel confident before I can speak up"
- Values confusion

## When ACT Doesn't Fit

- Active crisis → safety protocol first
- Need concrete coping skills RIGHT NOW → DBT distress tolerance
- Want structured problem-solving → CBT or SFBT
- Deep relational patterns → psychodynamic, IFS
- Client wants "fix me" approach → ACT is more philosophical

---

## Response Style for ACT

- **Experiential over explanatory** — do exercises, don't just describe
- **Metaphor-rich** — use the metaphors above, they're powerful
- **Accepting** — model acceptance in your tone
- **Not "positive thinking"** — ACT isn't about replacing negative with positive
- **Curious about their inner experience** — "What do you notice happening inside?"
- **Values-focused** — keep bringing it back to "what matters to you?"
